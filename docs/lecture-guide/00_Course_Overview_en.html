Multi-Agent Team Harness Engineering Workshop

From prompt engineering to harness engineering. A two-day hands-on workshop where you design and operate multi-agent teams across four tools: Claude Code, Claude App, Antigravity, and Antigravity CLI.

What is this workshop? Starting from the premise that "prompt engineering alone is not enough," this two-day hands-on workshop teaches a design methodology for multi-agent teams in which AI agents are given narrow roles and coordinated by an orchestrator, while participants actually build subagents and pipelines across the four tools: Claude Code, Claude App, Antigravity, and Antigravity CLI.

Day 1 targets general users and focuses on multi-agent concepts, hands-on practice with the tools, and using existing variants. Day 2 targets IT professionals and covers architecture in depth, tool comparison, enterprise operating strategy, creating new variants, and a capstone project.

🎯Learning Objectives

Upon completing this workshop, participants will be able to:

LO-1
Explain the definition of harness engineering in comparison with prompt and context engineering, and identify the three structural limitations of a single agent (context overload, role conflict, lack of parallelism).
LO-2
Design a multi-agent team composed of an orchestrator, specialists, and handoff contracts, and select the appropriate pattern from Anthropic's five orchestration patterns (chaining, routing, parallelization, orchestrator-worker, evaluator-optimizer) for a given situation.
LO-3
Apply a guardrail system to subagents that includes a three-tier classification of risky actions (read/write/destructive), the principle of least privilege, human approval gates, isolation, and audit logging.
LO-4
Execute subagent definition and invocation across the four tools — Claude Code, Claude App, Antigravity, and Antigravity CLI — and directly implement sequential pipelines and parallel execution.
LO-5
Compare the four tools' subagent definition methods, teammateMode, hook support, and Agent Manager differences, based on hands-on experience.
LO-6
Judge which of the two enterprise multi-agent team operating models (service-ticket model vs. development-tool + variant model) suits your organization and explain why.
LO-7
Implement your understanding of the ai-workspace-standards repository's L0→L1→L2 layer structure and its four core files by prototyping a new variant (co-marketing) and writing a promotion checklist.
LO-8
Synthesize your own recurring task into a problem statement, then design and execute the full cycle: problem definition → guardrail design → subagent implementation → verification and retrospective.

👥Audience

This workshop runs over two days, each aimed at a different audience. You may attend a single day that matches your background, or attend both days for a systematic learning path.

📅 Day 1 — General Users

Aimed at practitioners who want to apply AI tools to their work. Extensive coding experience is not required — familiarity with basic CLI operations is sufficient to follow along.

  • Practitioners newly adopting or already using AI coding tools daily. If you use Claude Code, GitHub Copilot, Cursor, or similar tools but feel limited by single-prompt interactions, this is for you.
  • Team leads or planners considering AI agent adoption. For those thinking through how to deploy agents within their team.
  • Instructors and consultants who teach AI tools. For those who cover Claude Code and Antigravity in their training materials.
Note: The Day 1 warm-up exercise (Tetris/Pac-Man) uses claude.ai or Claude Code. You can participate with prompt input alone even without coding experience, though familiarity with browser environments will make the HTML Canvas-based game exercise smoother.

📅 Day 2 — IT Professionals

Building on the Day 1 experience, this session targets IT professionals and covers architecture and deeper technical material.

  • Software engineers who use AI coding tools daily. If you use Claude Code, GitHub Copilot, Cursor, or similar tools but need experience designing multi-agent architectures, this is for you.
  • DevOps and platform engineers. For those whose work includes building organization-wide AI workspaces and managing variants.
  • Tech leads designing AI agent adoption. For those responsible for deploying agents safely across a whole team.
Note: This workshop focuses on the Claude Code and Antigravity family of tools, and assumes familiarity with installing and using the basic CLI operations of all four tools. Beginners without CLI experience are encouraged to thoroughly practice the preparatory steps in the installation guide beforehand.

Hands-on environment

All four tools (Claude App, Claude Code, Antigravity Desktop, Antigravity CLI) are installed, and participants are split into four groups, each running the same scenario on a different tool. At the end, results are compared, giving direct, first-hand experience of the content in the "tool comparison" chapter.

🕐Course Schedule

Total duration across both days is about 11 hours 15 minutes (excluding breaks). With 2 breaks on Day 1 and 2 breaks on Day 2 (10-15 minutes each), that comes to roughly 5h 55m-6h 25m on Day 1 and 6h-6h 30m on Day 2.

Scheduling principles

Day 1 (general users) focuses on harness engineering concepts, guardrails, and hands-on tool practice, introduces ai-workspace-standards lightly, and then dedicates ample time to hands-on practice with existing variants. It is structured so that participants with little coding experience can follow along. Day 2 (IT professionals) builds on Day 1 to cover the ai-workspace-standards architecture in depth, tool comparison, and enterprise operating strategy, then works through creating a new variant and a capstone project.

✅What You'll Gain After Completion

  • Multi-agent team design skills. Master the design methodology for transitioning from a single agent to a team structure, ready for immediate application to your own work.
  • Hands-on experience with all four tools. Run the same scenario yourself across Claude Code, Claude App, Antigravity, and the Antigravity command-line interface (CLI), and directly experience the strengths and weaknesses of each.
  • A guardrail design checklist. Take away a practical guardrail design template that covers risky-action classification, least privilege, and approval gates.
  • A variant prototype. Build a co-marketing prototype yourself based on ai-workspace-standards, giving you a concrete starting point for introducing variants within your organization.
  • Criteria for choosing an operating model. Compare the pros and cons of the service-ticket model and the variant model so you can settle on an operating strategy that fits your organization.
  • Your own workflow. In the capstone lab, reframe one of your own recurring tasks as a multi-agent team problem and experience the full cycle from problem definition through verification.
